T8 SEO FAQ

Frequently asked questions about the KEENON T8 before requesting a demo.

We answer the main questions to decide whether this compact waiter robot fits your hotel, restaurant, hospital, large facility or logistics flow.

Which restaurants is the KEENON T8 waiter robot suitable for?

The T8 is a compact option for restaurants, cafés, hotels and dining rooms that want to cut trips carrying plates and drinks without taking up too much space.

Does the T8 replace waiters?

No. The T8 works as operational support: it moves trays, cuts repetitive trips and lets staff spend more time on customer service, sales and the dining experience.

What is analysed before deploying a T8?

Alpha Robotics reviews kitchen-to-floor distances, aisle width, the number of tables, shifts, plate volume and time-saving goals.

Can I compare the T8 with the T9 or T10?

Yes. The assessment can compare the T8, T9, T10 and T11 by capacity, visual presence, dining-room size, type of service and expected return.

Operational roadmap

  1. Days 1-2Deployment and testing: delivery, unpacking and environment preparation.
  2. Days 3-4Integration and validation of the hotel's lifts and automated systems.
  3. Days 4-6Training and simulation with staff.
  4. Days 6-10Final go-live in real operating hours.

Capabilities, requirements and limitations

  • F&B T range: payload up to 40 kg depending on the model for floor service, buffet, clearing and kitchen-to-floor support.
  • Integration/connectivity: W3, S100 and the C range certified for multi-floor integration; autonomous use of lifts and automatic doors via a dedicated network and IoT (LoRaWAN/radio frequency), without interfering with guest WiFi.

Maintenance and SLA

  • Permanent technical support, parts, labour, technician call-outs and consumables included in the OPEX model.
  • In the event of a critical breakdown that compromises operations: replacement of the robot with a new or courtesy unit at no additional cost.
