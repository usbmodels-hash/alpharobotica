Technology and return

LiDAR, ROI and comparison tools to choose the right robot.

Three tools to make an informed decision before deploying robotics.

LiDAR: what it is and why it matters

KEENON robots combine 360-degree LiDAR navigation with safety sensors to build accurate maps of the environment using LiDAR and vision (VSLAM) fusion; cameras are used solely for on-board navigation and do not record or store images of guests or staff. This technology improves privacy, works even in low light and detects obstacles with high precision. When tables, trolleys or furniture change position, the system updates navigation and adapts routes to keep the operation stable.

ROI: how to measure return

The return from a service robot is not limited to direct labour-hour savings. In a hotel or restaurant there are three levers: staff hours released from repetitive tasks, fewer delays and operating errors, and constant availability during nights, holidays or demand peaks. Measuring these factors before buying helps decide whether to start with cleaning, F&B, room service or internal logistics.

Commercial and technical FAQ

Frequently asked questions about KEENON robots, pricing, installation and ROI.

These answers help decide whether a measurable pilot makes sense for your hotel, restaurant or operating site.

The questions we are asked before deploying a robot. If you do not find yours, write to us on WhatsApp.

Do the robots need Wi-Fi to work?

Not necessarily. KEENON robots have local navigation logic that works offline. Wi-Fi improves centralized management and updates, but the robot operates autonomously even without an active connection.

How long does installation take?

Standard deployment (mapping, setup and training, without lift integration) is completed in 1 to 3 days, with no construction work or magnetic tape. When the project includes lift and multi-floor automation integration, typical deployment is 6 to 10 days after the assessment.

What happens if the robot finds an unexpected obstacle?

The safety system combines LiDAR, 3D stereo vision, ultrasonic sensors and RGB cameras. The robot detects obstacles, slows down, replans the route and stops if there is no alternative path.

Who provides technical support in Spain?

Alpha Robotics provides local technical support: direct assistance, on-site visits when needed and software updates coordinated with KEENON. You do not depend on remote support without local assistance.

Does it work on carpets or uneven floors?

Delivery robots operate on standard hard floors. KLEENBOT C40 works on short-pile carpet up to 10 mm in dry mode (sweeping and vacuuming) and scrubs hard floors: marble, vinyl, epoxy and granite. The C55 is designed for large hard-floor areas. Ramps or uneven floors must be validated during the assessment.

What ROI should be expected?

It depends on the area. In cleaning, typical payback can be 12 to 18 months depending on surface and frequency. In F&B and room service, impact is measured by eliminated trips and released hours.

Do I need to hire someone to operate the robot?

No. Robots are operated from an app or from the robot screen. Staff training usually takes a few hours, and any employee can send the robot to a task with a few taps.

Does it work with multiple robots at once?

Yes. KEENON's multi-robot dispatching system coordinates fleets with route-planning algorithms, avoiding conflicts and optimizing task distribution.
