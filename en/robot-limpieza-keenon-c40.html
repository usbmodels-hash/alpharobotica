The C40 in action

The KLEENBOT C40 is the best-selling autonomous cleaning robot in the KEENON range, designed for facilities with areas over 500 m². In a single pass it combines sweeping and scrubbing: it collects solid debris, applies clean water to the floor and vacuums the dirty water into separate clean- and dirty-water tanks, avoiding leaving the floor wet and slippery. Its 360º LiDAR navigation system builds a map of the space during installation and updates it in real time if it detects obstacles or furniture changes. It runs in programmable cycles: it can start at 2 a.m. when the hotel is quiet, complete its route through the lobby, floor corridors and pool area, return to its charging dock on its own and be ready for a second shift. In hotels of 150 to 250 rooms, the C40 can cover all common areas in under 3 hours of continuous, unsupervised operation.

Frequently asked questions about the KLEENBOT C40

The questions we get before deploying a C40. If you can't find yours, message us on WhatsApp.

Can the C40 clean carpeted floors or rugs?

The KLEENBOT C40 can work on short-pile carpet up to 10 mm in dry mode (sweeping, vacuuming and dust mopping) thanks to its triple-brush system with dry-wet separation. Wet scrubbing is intended for hard floors only (marble, tile, vinyl, epoxy, granite). If your facility combines carpet and hard floors, the robot switches modes by map zone, with no component changes.

What happens if the dirty-water tank fills up during operation?

The robot detects the tank level in real time. When it reaches 90% of its capacity, it pauses cleaning, returns to its emptying dock (if installed) or sends an alert to the management panel so the operator can empty it. Once emptied, it resumes the route from where it left off.

Can the C40 clean several different areas in a single shift?

Yes. During installation, independent cleaning zones are defined (lobby, floor corridors, pool area, etc.) and scheduled in the order and timing that best suits operations. The robot moves between zones autonomously following the programmed sequence.

How long does the initial mapping of a hotel take?

Initial mapping depends on the area and complexity of the space. In mid-size hotels (1,000 to 3,000 m² of common areas), the full mapping and configuration process is completed in 1 to 2 working days. Alpha Robotics handles the entire installation and commissioning process, included in the service.

Does the C40 detect people moving and stop automatically?

Yes. The 360º LiDAR system and ultrasonic sensors detect moving obstacles, including people, and the robot slows down or stops and finds an alternative route. It never collides with people or furniture. Navigation safety is certified under CE regulations.

Does the C40 need constant supervision during cleaning?

No. Once the map and routes are set up, the robot can start its cleaning cycles on schedule, avoid obstacles, alert you if it needs intervention and return to its dock. The team only needs to check tanks, consumables and occasional incidents.
