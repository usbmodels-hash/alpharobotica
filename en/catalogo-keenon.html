W3 + S100: private deliveries, amenities and multi-floor service.

Room service operations in hotels can combine two complementary flows: the W3 for private, enclosed deliveries to the room, and the S100 adapted as a bellhop service for light luggage, bulky amenities and visible support between floors.

W3 · Private deliveries

BUTLERBOT W3 for room service, amenities and enclosed orders.

The W3 works in corridors and rooms with enclosed compartments, lift integration and discreet door delivery. It's the main option for private orders, night room service and amenities that require traceability.

  • Enclosed compartments for guest deliveries.
  • Multi-floor integration with lifts.
  • Silent, autonomous operation 24/7.

Frequently asked questions for choosing the right KEENON robot.

We answer the usual questions for choosing a family and model. If you need a quick recommendation, message us on WhatsApp.

Which robot is best to start with in a 100-room hotel?

It depends on the problem you want to solve first. If the biggest bottleneck is cleaning common areas, the C40 is the most common starting point. If it's night room service, the W3. If it's the floor team's workload at breakfast or dinner, a DINERBOT T9 or T10. In the free assessment we help you identify which gives the highest return in your specific operation.

Can I have robots from different families working at the same time in the same hotel?

Yes, and it's standard in medium and large hotels. A 150-room hotel can have a C40 for night cleaning, a W3 for room service and two DINERBOT T10s in the restaurant. The robots don't interfere with each other because each family operates in different areas of the hotel.

Is there any KEENON robot that can do both room service and cleaning?

No. Each family is designed and optimised for a specific operational function. Trying to make one robot do multiple functions would reduce its efficiency in each. The KEENON catalogue's architecture of specialised families is precisely what guarantees the return on investment per operational area.

What is the difference between the C40 and the C55? When is the C55 worth it?

The C55 has more scrubbing power, a larger tank and is designed for surfaces over 2,000 m² per shift. If your facility is a medium hotel or care home, the C40 is usually enough. For shopping centres, airports, logistics or very large surfaces, the C55 can cut cycles and improve the cost per square metre.

What information does Alpha Robotics need to recommend a robot?

We need to know the surface area, the number of rooms or tables, current shifts, aisle width, floor type, whether there are lifts, the workflow and the financial goal. With that data we prepare an initial assessment and a phased deployment recommendation.

Which robot fits your operation first?

Alpha Robotics' free assessment identifies the flow with the highest return and turns the KEENON catalogue into an operational decision: which robot, for which area, with what goal and over what payback period.
