How much does an autonomous cleaning robot cost in 2026?

Indicative price, hidden costs, ROI and payback for hotels, hospitals and large facilities.

Frequently asked questions

How much does an autonomous cleaning robot cost?

As a guide, the KLEENBOT C30 starts at €8,900, the C40 at €12,500 and the C55 at €18,900 (+VAT). They can also be taken on renting (RaaS) with Grenke as a monthly fee. The final price is on request depending on surface area, configuration and services.

Is it better to buy or rent (RaaS) a cleaning robot?

A direct purchase is more cost-effective in the long run; renting or RaaS avoids the upfront investment, includes maintenance and turns it into a monthly operating expense. The decision depends on your cash flow and usage horizon.

How much do you save per month with a cleaning robot?

The saving comes from the cleaning hours freed up multiplied by their labour cost, minus the cost of the robot and consumables. It varies a lot by surface area and shifts; the ROI calculator estimates it with your real figures.

What is the payback of a cleaning robot?

It depends on the surface area, the shifts and the labour cost. With intensive cleaning it's usually reached in the first months of use; in lower-intensity scenarios it takes longer. The calculator gives you the exact figure for your facility.

When a hotel chain, a shopping centre or a facility manager asks how much an autonomous cleaning robot costs, the right answer shouldn't start with the purchase price. The important figure is the total cost of operation: how much cleaning costs today, how many repetitive hours the team spends, which areas are cleaned most often and how much value is created by freeing staff for more judgement-based tasks.

A robot like the KLEENBOT isn't deployed to replace an entire operation but to absorb the most repetitive routes: corridors, common areas, lobbies, open surfaces, supermarket aisles, hospitals or logistics areas. That's why the price must be analysed alongside the number of square metres, shifts, cleaning frequency and real labour cost.

Hidden costs no one tells you about before buying

Installation and initial mapping

The robot needs to learn the environment, set up zones, routes, charging points and restrictions. This work is key: a good map avoids incidents and improves productivity from day one.

Team training

Staff must learn to send tasks, change consumables, check alerts and coordinate the robot with daily operations. Training reduces internal friction and prevents the robot from ending up underused.

Annual preventive maintenance

Brushes, squeegees, filters, sensors and tanks need checking. A well-defined preventive maintenance plan protects the investment and avoids unnecessary downtime in peak season.

Software updates

Navigation, maps and fleet management evolve. Having local support helps maintain performance and adapt routes when the layout of the space changes.

How to calculate the real ROI over 3 years

Let's imagine a 150-room hotel with three common-area cleaning shifts. If each shift spends two hours a day on repetitive routes, that's six hours a day. At 22 operating days a month, that's 132 hours a month. With a total labour cost of €16 an hour, the associated monthly cost exceeds €2,100.

If the robot absorbs 60% of that repetitive load, it frees up about 79 hours a month. Over a year that's almost 950 hours the team can devote to supervision, detail cleaning, rooms, incidents or attending to quality standards. The direct saving isn't the only variable: omissions are reduced, consistency improves and a constant standard can be maintained in low-availability slots.

Over a three-year horizon, compare the upfront investment, maintenance, consumables and electricity against the labour cost avoided or reassigned. The ROI will be higher the larger the surface area, the more frequent the cleaning and the more repetitive the routes.

When does the robot start to pay off?

The payback period is the time it takes for the accumulated saving to equal the investment. Taking a C40 from €12,500 + VAT as a reference and adding deployment, training, accessories and support, the real project is usually analysed as a full investment above the base price. If the operation frees up between €1,200 and €1,500 net a month between reassigned hours and operational improvements, the return can be around 12 to 18 months, depending on shifts, surface area and frequency of use. In facilities with more shifts or larger surfaces, the payback can be shorter.

The key is not to buy from a catalogue. First the flow is validated: metres, schedules, floors, water points, foot traffic, lifts where applicable and the team's capacity to integrate the robot into their routine.
