CAPEX vs OPEX/RaaS

KEENON robot acquisition models: CAPEX, finance leasing and OpExFlow.

The commercial decision should be kept separate from the technical one. You can buy the robot outright, finance it as a CAPEX asset or take out an OpExFlow service with no ownership and support included.

See the CAPEX optionSee the OpExFlow option

Clear comparison

Don't mix finance leasing with OpExFlow.

Both routes can be paid monthly, but they follow different logics in accounting, operational and contractual terms.

ModelWho it suitsOwnershipHow it's interpreted
Direct purchaseLocal businesses with capital available.YesPure CAPEX.
Finance leasingRestaurants, cafés and SMEs that prefer to finance.Yes, subject to final terms.CAPEX financed through a lender.
Traditional leasingCompanies that want to plan the asset with financing.Yes, if the agreed purchase option is exercised.CAPEX with a financing schedule.

Practical decision

Choose by scale, operational risk and in-house capacity.

The technology can be the same; what changes is how you take on investment, maintenance, continuity and operational responsibility.

CAPEX / ownership

Food service and local businesses.

Purchase, finance leasing or leasing with final ownership. Recommended when the business wants to control the asset and start with a contained rollout.

See the CAPEX funnel

OpExFlow / service

Hotels and large accounts.

€0 upfront, a monthly fee, SLA, consumables included and replacement on critical failure. Recommended when continuity matters more than ownership.
