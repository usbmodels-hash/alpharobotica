Capabilities, requirements and limitations

  • F&B T range: payload up to 40 kg depending on the model for floor service, buffet, clearing and kitchen-to-floor support.
  • Integration/connectivity: W3, S100 and the C range certified for multi-floor integration; autonomous use of lifts and automatic doors via a dedicated network and IoT (LoRaWAN/radio frequency), without interfering with guest WiFi.

Maintenance and SLA

  • Permanent technical support, parts, labour, technician call-outs and consumables included in the OPEX model.
  • In the event of a critical breakdown that compromises operations: replacement of the robot with a new or courtesy unit at no additional cost.

Frequently asked questions about the KEENON T9

The questions we get before deploying a KEENON T9. If you can't find yours, message us on WhatsApp.

How much does the KEENON T9 waiter robot cost?

The T9 is available as a RaaS model (fixed monthly fee, no upfront investment) or as a direct purchase on request. The price depends on the configuration and units; calculate an estimate in the configurator or request a free assessment.

Can the T9 be used to clear dirty tableware?

Yes. The T9 is particularly suited to buffet, the kitchen-to-floor link and tableware clearing, with round trips that take repetitive work off the team at peak hours.

How much weight does the T9 take and what is its battery life?

It carries up to 40 kg on its 4 adjustable 10 kg trays, moves at 0.8 m/s and offers up to 18 hours of battery life with a 4-hour charge. It fits through aisles from 70 cm.

What is the lead time for the T9?

The indicative lead time is a few weeks from confirmation, depending on stock and configuration, and includes route setup and training. The exact date is set during the assessment.

What installation requirements does the T9 need?

Stable WiFi, a power socket for the charging dock, aisles with enough room and an initial mapping of the dining room. We validate the route in a prior analysis.
