Capabilities, requirements and limitations

  • F&B T range: payload up to 40 kg depending on the model for floor service, buffet, clearing and kitchen-to-floor support.
  • Integration/connectivity: W3, S100 and the C range certified for multi-floor integration; autonomous use of lifts and automatic doors via a dedicated network and IoT (LoRaWAN/radio frequency), without interfering with guest WiFi.

Maintenance and SLA

  • Permanent technical support, parts, labour, technician call-outs and consumables included in the OPEX model.
  • In the event of a critical breakdown that compromises operations: replacement of the robot with a new or courtesy unit at no additional cost.

Frequently asked questions about the KEENON T10

The questions we get before deploying a KEENON T10. If you can't find yours, message us on WhatsApp.

How much does the KEENON T10 waiter robot cost?

The T10 is offered as a RaaS model or a direct purchase on request. The price varies with configuration and the number of units; get an estimate in the configurator or request a free, no-obligation assessment.

What does the T10 add compared with more basic models?

The T10 is geared towards premium F&B and the guest experience, combining floor transport with a polished presence and interaction through screens. It's a common choice in hotels and restaurants that care about their brand image.

How much weight and battery life does the T10 offer?

It takes up to 40 kg, moves at 1 m/s and offers up to 8 hours of battery life with a 5.5-hour charge. It adds an 11.6-inch operation screen and a 23.8-inch advertising screen.

What is the lead time for the T10?

The indicative lead time for delivery and deployment is a few weeks from confirmation, depending on stock and configuration, and includes setup and training.

What installation requirements does the T10 need?

It needs stable WiFi, a socket for the charging dock, aisles with enough width and an initial mapping. We carry out a prior analysis of the premises.
